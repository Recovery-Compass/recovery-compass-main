# Settlement Proposal Template - Attorney Fee Dispute

**Use Case:** Resolving attorney fee dispute without litigation  
**Applicable:** *Marriage of Borson* fee motions, billing disputes, termination for cause  
**Framework:** Professional, factual, face-saving for attorney

---

## Template Structure

### Subject Line
> Re: [Case Name] - Settlement Proposal for Attorney Fee Dispute

### Opening Paragraph (Professional Tone)
> Dear [Attorney Name],
>
> I represent [Client Name] in connection with your November [X], 2025 *Borson* fee motion requesting $[Amount] in attorney fees. I write to propose a settlement that resolves this dispute efficiently and preserves the professional relationships involved.

### Context Paragraph (Neutral Facts)
> As you know, you represented [Client] from [Start Date] to [End Date] ([X] days). Your invoices total $[Amount], which you now seek from [Opposing Party] under *Marriage of Borson*.

### Audit Summary (Factual, Non-Adversarial)
> I have completed a comprehensive billing audit comparing your invoices to California State Bar billing guidelines and industry standards for [Practice Area] attorneys in [County]. The audit identified $[X]-[Y] in fees that may not meet the "reasonable fees" standard under Rule 1.5(a).

### Violation Categories (Numbered, Specific)
> The audit found the following issues:
>
> 1. **Block Billing:** [X] entries combining multiple distinct tasks, preventing verification of time spent per task (*Christian Research Institute v. Alnor*, 165 Cal. App. 4th 1315).
>
> 2. **Vague Descriptions:** [X] entries with insufficient detail to assess reasonableness (*Ketchum v. Moses*, 24 Cal. 4th 1122).
>
> 3. **Excessive Time:** Several routine tasks exceed industry standards by [X]%-[Y]%, particularly [specific examples].
>
> 4. **Post-Termination Work:** $[X] billed for work performed after termination date, including fees for drafting your own fee motion.
>
> 5. **Communication Failures:** [Date] email from client with [X] urgent requests went unanswered, violating Rule 1.4(a)(4).
>
> [Optional 6. Third-Party Payor Issues: Rule 1.8.6 disclosure not obtained from third-party financial supporter.]
>
> [Optional 7. UPL Concerns: [Non-attorney name] participated in meetings providing legal advice without proper paralegal licensing/supervision.]

### Justified Fee Calculation (Objective)
> Based on industry standards for [Practice Area] in [County] (hourly rates: $[X]-[Y]; task time allocations per [Source]), the justified fee range for your [X]-day representation is $[A]-[B].

**Calculation Summary:**
| Task Category | Hours Billed | Justified Hours | Rate | Justified Amount |
|---------------|--------------|-----------------|------|------------------|
| [Task 1] | [X] | [Y] | $[Rate] | $[Amount] |
| [Task 2] | [X] | [Y] | $[Rate] | $[Amount] |
| **TOTAL** | **[X]** | **[Y]** | **$[Rate]** | **$[Total]** |

### Settlement Offer (Clear, Specific)
> To resolve this matter without further litigation, [Client] proposes the following settlement terms:
>
> 1. **Payment:** $[Amount] (representing justified fees for work performed)
> 2. **Timing:** Payment within [X] days of agreement execution
> 3. **Withdrawal:** You withdraw the *Borson* fee motion with prejudice
> 4. **Release:** Mutual release of all claims related to representation
> 5. **Confidentiality:** [Optional - if desired] Terms remain confidential except as required by law

### Face-Saving Language (Professional Courtesy)
> I recognize that billing disputes often arise from good-faith differences in assessing reasonableness. This proposal is not intended to impugn your professional reputation, but rather to resolve the matter based on objective industry standards.

### Deadline (Reasonable Pressure)
> Please respond by [Date] at [Time]. If we cannot reach agreement, I will file an opposition brief on [Date due], which will detail the billing violations identified above and request the court reduce your fee award to $[A]-[B] or deny the motion entirely.

### Alternative Dispute Resolution (Optional)
> If you believe the audit contains factual errors, I am open to:
> - **Mediation:** Joint session with neutral mediator
> - **Binding Arbitration:** Submit fee dispute to arbitrator for final determination
> - **Fee Expert Review:** Agree on neutral billing expert to assess reasonableness

### Professional Closing
> I believe this proposal is fair and reasonable. I look forward to your response.
>
> Respectfully,
>
> [Attorney Name]  
> [Firm Name]  
> [Email]  
> [Phone]

## Strategic Notes

### When to Use Settlement
**Best Candidates:**
- Fee dispute is $5,000-$50,000 (worth negotiating but not worth prolonged litigation)
- Violations are clear but attorney may not realize extent
- Client wants matter resolved quickly
- Attorney's reputation at stake (State Bar complaint possible)

**Not Appropriate:**
- Egregious violations (fraud, theft, gross misconduct)
- Attorney has pattern of similar conduct
- Client wants public accountability
- Fee amount too small to justify settlement negotiation

### Tone Calibration
**Professional, Not Personal:**
- Focus on billing standards, not attorney's character
- Use passive voice: "The audit found..." not "You committed..."
- Cite authority (cases, rules, standards) not opinion
- Offer face-saving explanation ("good-faith differences")

**Firm, Not Aggressive:**
- Clear on violations and consequences
- Specific numbers and deadlines
- Alternative path (opposition brief) explicitly stated
- No threats beyond normal litigation process

### Settlement Amount Strategy
**Range Approach:**
- Calculate justified fees objectively
- Offer at top of justified range (not bottom)
- Shows reasonableness and good faith
- Attorney saves face by accepting "fair" amount

**Face-Saving Framing:**
> "$7,000 represents the reasonable value of services performed for [Client's] benefit."
> 
> *Not:* "$7,000 because you overcharged $9,000."

### Negotiation Tactics
**If Attorney Counters:**
- Listen to objections (some audit points may be incorrect)
- Adjust if legitimate errors found
- Stand firm on clear violations
- Propose meet-in-the-middle if close

**If Attorney Refuses:**
- Proceed with opposition brief as stated
- Do not threaten State Bar complaint in settlement letter (wait until post-hearing)
- Follow through on all stated consequences

**If Attorney Ignores:**
- Send follow-up email 2 days before deadline
- If still no response, proceed with opposition brief
- Note non-response in brief ("Despite good-faith settlement offer...")

## Evidence to Attach (Optional)

**Consider Including:**
1. **Billing Audit Summary:** 1-page table showing violations and justified amounts
2. **Industry Standards Reference:** Citation to State Bar guidelines or CLA salary survey
3. **Sample Violation:** 1-2 specific invoice entries with analysis

**Do NOT Include:**
- Full opposition brief (save for court)
- Personal attacks or character evidence
- Threats beyond normal litigation
- State Bar complaint threats (premature)

---

## Timeline Example

**Day 1:** Send settlement proposal with 5-7 day response deadline  
**Day 3:** Follow-up email if no acknowledgment  
**Day 5:** Final reminder of deadline  
**Day 6-7:** Response deadline  
**Day 8 (if accepted):** Draft settlement agreement  
**Day 10:** Execute settlement, payment received, motion withdrawn  
**Day 11+ (if rejected):** File opposition brief per original deadline

## Success Metrics

**Settlement Success Rate:** 40-60% (many attorneys prefer settlement over litigation)  
**Average Settlement:** 60-80% of original fee request (saving 20-40%)  
**Time Saved:** 10-20 hours of opposition brief drafting, hearing prep, hearing attendance  
**Cost Saved:** $2,500-10,000 in legal fees (depending on attorney rates)
